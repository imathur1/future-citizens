Green Cards

There are several ways to obtain a Green Card, the de facto document of permanent residence in the United States, each suited for different situations and circumstances:

1) Family Based Visa

Fiancée Visa

When a non-US citizen marries another US citizen, they may apply for permanent residency with the intent of building a new life with their partner in the United States.

Parent Visa

A US citizen over the age of 21 may apply for their parents to become permanent residents of the US.

Child/Sibling Visa

The child/sibling of a US citizen may also apply for permanent residency.

2) Employment Based Visa

Employer Sponsorship

With this type of visa, an employer hires a forign employee, on the condition that they are unable to find a US Citizen of equal qualification. After the hire, the employer may petition for the employee to receive a green card if they are satisfied with their quality of work. They MUST be a full time employee who is paid at least the same as an equivalent US Citizen.

  • EB2 → Visa for Petition of Skilled Labor/Advanced Positions
  • EB3 → Visa for Petition of Unskilled Labor

3) Self Petition Visa

National Interest Waiver

Though not strictly defined, aliens who work in a US industry which greatly benefits from their employment may self-petition for permanent residency under the EB2 Visa.

Investor Visa

An alien who invests a large sum of money into the economy ($900,000) which directly creates jobs in the United States may also self-petition for permanent residency under the EB5 Visa.

Extraordinary Ability

  • When a non-US citizen archives extraordinary status/success, they may apply for permanent status under the EB1A Visa.
    • Examples include millionaires, authors, professional athletes, etc.
  • EB1B → For outstanding professors and researchers with accolades, publications, etc.
  • EB1C → For multinational executives or managers of companies that have worked at the company for at least 1 year, who wish to transfer to the US.

4) Diversity Visa

Under this type of visa, a set number of green cards are awarded to aliens from underrepresented countries in the United States. They may apply to be placed in a lottery which determines whether they get the green card or not.

5) Extenuating Circumstances

T-Visa

This visa is offered to those who are victims of human trafficking.

U-Visa

This visa is offered to those who suffer from physical or mental abuse, who may be beneficial to law enforcement in investigating criminal activity.

VAWA

This visa was designed for spouses, parents, or children who have been subject to abuse by another US Citizen.
