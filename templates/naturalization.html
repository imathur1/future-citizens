Naturalization

Following the obtainment of a Green Card through one of the processes outlined above, a permanent resident must satisfy the requirements of Normalization in order to become a US Citizen. Naturalization is the term coined by the US Government for the process of obtaining citizenship. The requirements and steps are as follows:

1) Naturalization Requirements

  1. Must be 18 years of age or older.
  2. Must have a Green Card for 5+ years (3+ if married to a citizen).
  3. Must physically reside in the US for 2.5+ years of the 5 in the US (1.5 if married to a citizen).
  4. Cannot stay internationally for 6+ months during the 5 years (3 years if married to a citizen).
  5. Must reside in the same state for 3+ months prior to filing for naturalization.
  6. Must exhibit good moral character – specific criminal activity is not excused.
  7. Must be literate in English, pass a proficiency test, and have a basic understanding of English.
  8. Must pass the US Government & Civics Test which asks questions about US government/history.
  9. Must take Oath of Allegiance – defend the constitution, renounce citizenship to other countries.

2) Naturalization Application Process

  1. Fill out the N-400 form and submit to USCIS.
  2. Administer fingerprints and submit to the US Government.
  3. Naturalization interview with a USCIS officer.
  4. Pass the English Proficiency Test as mentioned above.
  5. Pass the Civics Test with a minimum 60%.
